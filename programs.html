Our Programs

We work to build resilience, strengthen communities, and support sustainable development across Jubbaland.

Water & Sanitation

Providing clean water access, sanitation facilities, and hygiene education.

Food Security

Supporting families with emergency food aid and long‑term agricultural resilience.

Education & Youth

Empowering youth through education, skills training, and mentorship.

Health & Nutrition

Delivering essential health services and nutrition support for families.

Water & Sanitation

Access to clean water is essential for health, dignity, and development. SolidaritySom works with communities to rehabilitate wells, install water systems, and promote hygiene practices that reduce disease and improve quality of life.

  • Rehabilitation of community wells
  • Construction of latrines and sanitation facilities
  • Hygiene awareness campaigns
  • Water trucking during emergencies

Food Security

We support vulnerable families with emergency food assistance while also investing in long‑term agricultural solutions that strengthen resilience and reduce dependency.

  • Emergency food distribution
  • Support for farmers and pastoralists
  • Climate‑smart agriculture training
  • Nutrition awareness programs

Education & Youth

Education is the foundation of opportunity. We work to ensure children and youth have access to safe learning spaces and the skills needed to build a better future.

  • School rehabilitation and supplies
  • Youth leadership programs
  • Vocational training
  • Teacher support and training

Health & Nutrition

We provide essential health services, maternal care, and nutrition support to improve community well‑being and reduce preventable illnesses.

  • Mobile health clinics
  • Maternal and child health services
  • Nutrition screening and supplements
  • Community health education

Women Economic Empowerment

The Solidarity Group of Jubilant implements community-based initiatives that empower women and vulnerable groups through vocational skills training, including:

  • Soap production, to enhance income generation
  • Resilience
  • Sustainable livelihoods in urban areas
  • Such as Kismayo

PROTECTION AND ADVOCACY

Protection violations that existed in 1991 continue, but have been exacerbated by the instability that the conflict in the states and engendered in other parts of Somalia. In addition, the conflict has led to new dynamics and incidents in protection violations

  • Linked to the armed conflict
  • Clan conflicts
  • Use of children in armed conflict
  • Warring parties to the current conflict in the country.
